Navigating My College Academics

October 13, 2024 by Jessica Beaudoin

My time in college has been a remarkable experience filled with academic exploration and personal dvelopment. A significant highlight of my journey has been discovering a major that resonates with my enthusiam for technology and creativity. I am in the process of obtaining a degree that will prepare me for a role in a corporate retail brand, where I aspire to design websites and enhance the technological elements of the business. The courses I have completed this far have provided me with the opportunity to delve into a diverse array of skills, encompassing web development, coding, marketing principles, and user experience design. I have particularly appreciated exploring HTML 5, as it has been essential in enhancing my grasp of front-end web development. Building organized, effective websites has ignited my passion for the technical aspects and has also guided me in tackling challenges with a rational approach. Juggling my academic duties alongside my passion for creativity has proven to be both a challenge and a fufilling experience. Every project I engage in, whether it's creating webpages or addressing case studies in digital marketing, represents a significant advancement toward my career aspirations. I look forward to furthering my understanding and utilizing my knowledge in practical situations as I get ready for life after college.

My Sorority Experience

October 13, 2024 by Jessica Beaudoin

Joining a sorority was one of the most life-changing things that I have done in college. When I got to campus for the first time, I wasn't sure if sorority life was right for me. It scared me to put myself out there, but something inside me told me to do it, and I'm so glad I did. It began as a way to meet new people and has grown into a group of strong, helpful women who inspire me every day. We all promise to give back, which is one of the things I love most about my sorority. We do a lot of volunteer work throughout the year, but I'm most proud of the work we do to raise money and awareness for women's heart health. It's a cause that really matters to me, and I feel proud and fulfilled when I know that we're making a real difference in people's lives. I feel empowered to contribute to something bigger than myself, whether it's planning events, raising money, or just teaching others. Aside from helping others, sorority life has also helped me grow as a person. The experience has made me who I am today, from giving me chances to lead to making friends for life. I'm grateful for the confidence and sense of belonging my sorority has given me. The values of sisterhood, service, and personal growth have become important parts of who I am. This group is more than just an organization; it's like a family, and I can't imagine college without it.
